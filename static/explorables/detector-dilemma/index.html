When detection breaks down

We used to spot machine-written text at a glance — it had a certain tinniness. As the models improved that tell faded, so we handed the job to detectors that score how "machine-like" a passage reads and flag anything past a cutoff. Step through what happens to that cutoff as the machine learns to write like us.
